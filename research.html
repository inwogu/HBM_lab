Dataset Infant Anonymization with Pose and Emotion Retention (DIAPER)



DIAPER is a system focused on the anonymization of identities of infants in datasets. Many groups conducting experiments with babies generate datasets that could be used in other experiments. However, due to parental consent, much of this data is restricted to only a single research project or publication. Having the ability to anonymize baby faces while still retaining useful features would allow for the wider dissemination of infant datasets.
To do this, DIAPER utilizes DECA, a tool for turning 2D images of people into 3D models with separate controls for shape, pose, and emotion. By keeping pose and emotion locked, the shape can be freely changed to keep the identities of infants hidden. However, DECA is trained on adult heads, and therefore struggles with the shape of an infant head. Another tool called MICA, which turns 2D images into metrically accurate 3D models, is used to generate the shape component of the 3D model produced by DECA.



SignDict: From General Language to Sign Language



Sign language generation not only promotes communication between the hearing-impaired community and the general public but also further improves the mutual understanding between humans and robots. We propose SignDict, a text-to-sign video approach to deal with challenges in pose estimation and the design of generative networks to further improve the performance.
