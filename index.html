Human Behavior Modeling Lab is an academic lab that is uses artificial intelligence to study and design algorithms for behavior modeling. Research topics that the lab is currently working on are sign language detection, pose estimation and language processing. The lab is a part of Computer Science and Engineering department at University at Buffalo. The aim lab aims to bridge the gap between the theoretical models and their real life applications to assist mankind.

AtomVAE

Language-driven motion generation is an important task due to its wide application. To address the limitations of action generation on close-set, we propose AtomVAE for zero-shot text-to-motion synthesis, aiming to generate novel Unseen categories of actions. The Demo and code are on the way.

SignDict

Sign language generation not only promotes communication between the hearing-impaired community and the general public but also further improves the mutual understanding between humans and robots.

DIAPER

Reconstruction of infant faces with accurate shape, pose, and emotions using 3D morphable models. Allows for the anonymization of infants in various datasets.
